Accent Reduction

If English is an additional language for you and you have accented speech because of your native language, you might consider speech therapy for accent reduction. Several aspects of speaking are different across languages. Words are stressed differently, there might be sounds you have never used, , and sometimes sentences are produced with different intonation. Sounds in words, intonation, stress, and rhythm in speech are all targeted in therapy for accent reduction.

Stuttering

Stuttered speech is characterized by pauses, sounds repetitions, whole word repetitions, and sometimes extra body movements while speaking. Stuttering responds favorably to early intervention. Treatment in the preschool years shows the greatest success. Children often repeat sounds and words as they are learning to speak but some children get stuck in the word repetition cycle and this can develop into patterns of stuttering. Also there is some added evidence that stuttering has a genetic component. Often there is more than one family member who has stuttered speech. If you are concerned about your preschoolers speech and you are worried about stuttering it be is important to see a speech therapist as soon as possible.

Stuttering can be treated with electronic devices as well as therapy designed to alter movement patterns during speaking. If you are a teen or an adult and you stutter, the best thing to do is let your stuttering just happen. Trying to reduce stuttering by forcing the words out or hurrying to complete a sentence will make stuttering worse. Trying to hide stuttering by using different words or avoiding conversations will also make it worse.

Featured Therapists

Allison Baird

Allison Baird’s career spans almost 4 decades, and includes hospital based care, research, teaching, training and also private practice. Throughout her career, Allison has been focused on training and mentoring the next generation of speech therapists while ensuring she’s at the forefront of a rapidly changing field.

Allison has been a mentor to almost 40 young Speech-Language Pathologists. With her continued drive to improve her quality of care, she feels a strong responsibility to pass on her acquired knowledge to the next generation of speech therapists. At the same time she is exposed constantly to the newest ideas and technological advances that are happening in the university research settings.

Allison’s has worked extensively with children with severe disabilities and adults who have had a stroke, have been diagnosed with dementia, and people of all ages who stutter. Under the most difficult circumstances, Allison creates an environment where people thrive, have hope, and look forward to their next meeting.

Over the years Allison’s professional and personal experience with dementia has given her a deep understanding of the grief journey. She has channeled this experience to help her clients, and their families, to overcome seemingly insurmountable obstacles.

She has a spent half a decade pioneering tele-rehabilitation, a modality of service that allows people to receive speech therapy over the internet in the privacy of their own home. An invaluable tool for anyone with mobility issues or those living in remote communities. Allison has presented her work and research nationally and internationally and has received recognition for her work and research on the effectiveness of tele-rehabilitation for people who have had a stroke.

Pricing

Services provided by our registered Speech-Language Pathologists are covered by most private insurance plans.

Initial Assessment

$150 per 50 minutes
$100 per 30 minutes

The initial assessment is provided by a registered Speech-Language Pathologist. The purpose of the assessment is to diagnose the type of communication difficulty and determine the proper treatment plan. It usually takes 50 minutes to complete and the therapist will send you an report with a treatment plan within 5 business days.

Therapy Session

$110 per 50 minutes
$75 per 30 minutes

Each therapy session is provided by a registered Speech-Language Pathologist. The session is usually about 30 or 50 minutes. During the session, the therapist will monitor your progress, help you practice, and assign exercises to practice on your own during the week.

Supplement Session

$65 per 50 minutes
$45 per 30 minutes

The supplement sessions are provided by our trained Speech-Language Assistants. The treatment plan for each session is made by your primary therapist and services are monitored by your registered therapist. The Assistant will always monitor and report your progress to your therapist to make sure the treatment plan fits your needs.
